Conclusion

In conclusion, one’s employment and salary after college may be affected by their major, their college of choice, or even their gender. As shown in the first graph, those with Education majors are likely to get a job after college, while those with Social Sciences majors are less likely to. Furthermore, Business majors have very low chances of getting a job directly to their major, while Interdisciplinary majors have high chances, as shown in the third graph. In the fourth graph, we have seen that there are less women in majors that have been shown to be higher-paying. We have also learned, in the fifth graph, that those who go to colleges in California and the Northeast are very likely to score higher-paying jobs. Finally, the last four graphs have shown us that graduates from Ivy League and engineering schools consistently get higher-paying jobs than those from state, liberal arts, or party schools.
